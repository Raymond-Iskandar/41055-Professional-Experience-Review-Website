  • What were your expectations about your internship before you joined?

    • Before I joined, I was expecting that I would sit in a personal office and tasks would be given to me to work on them on a computer. Mainly, I was thinking about working in an independent space, supervised by my supervisor. I was also expecting to work in a company or work from home hybrid, given that I would look for a software engineering internship. When I was thinking of the Optik Consultancy internship, I was expecting to work on a certain project with different people from close disciplines to me.

  • What was the reality? How was it different from your expectations?

    • In reality, I ended up finding an internship at a school as an IT Helpdesk. The reality was far from what I had imagined. I ended up working in an IT Department room with 2 other people, and I used my own laptop to do my work. Moreover, I had to do work outside of the department all over the school to get the devices' information and so on. I expected there would be a heavy workload, but some days, the workload was fine and not too much. I have also ended up working with another intern at the same time.

  • What lessons were the most important from your internship. Why were they important?

    • The first lesson from my internship was not to take everything at work seriously, as working in a seriously all the time can lead to exhaustion and a decrease in productivity. It is important to be fun and easy-going during the job from time to time to break the daily routine.

    The second lesson was that not every person at work has the same tolerance as others. Some people can be friendly, and others can be serious. It's a matter of knowing how to deal with each person.

  • After your workplace experience, what would you say your value proposition would be to an employer? How can you demonstrate this?

    • To an employer, I would definitely bring professionalism, but most importantly, fun at the same time, adding to that good behaviour with my colleagues and staff. This was demonstrated during my internship when I had to be away from work for a while, and when I came back, everyone was asking about me and was happy to see me again, indicating that I had an impact on the school and the staff there during my internship.

  • How did your internship influence the type of role in which you are interested?

    • To be honest, the internship that I found does not relate to my field of study, nor to my future career path. I am mostly interested in the academic path. However, from this internship, I found out that doing IT stuff is not as fun as it seems, no matter how much experience you have or how quickly you can get the job done. This internship has given me a push into pursuing an academic career or working independently in the future, from home and not in an office with other people. Lastly, the interaction with hardware during my internship has increased my interest in computer engineering.
